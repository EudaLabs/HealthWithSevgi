## Instructor Notes / Guidance Visible on the Page

### Chapter 1 — AIMS Scope, Context & Stakeholders
This is the foundation of the whole report. Before you can govern an AI system, you have to define what it is and where its boundaries are. Think of this chapter as drawing a fence around your project.

- **1.1 — AI System Description:** simplest section. Five fields: what the system is called, what it does and for whom, which ML techniques it uses, how it's deployed, and what lifecycle stage it's in. The key point is the lifecycle stage field — write **"educational deployment, NOT clinical production."**
- **1.2 — AIMS Scope Statement:** the most important subsection in the entire report. The in-scope / out-of-scope table must draw explicit, justified boundaries. Every out-of-scope item needs a reason.
- **1.3 — Internal & External Issues:** maps to ISO Clause 4.1. External issues: EU AI Act, GDPR/KVKK, public concern about healthcare AI. Internal issues: team skills, no GPU, 10-week timeline, etc. Avoid vague statements.
- **1.4 — Stakeholder Register:** maps to ISO Clause 4.2. Include everyone affected by or interested in the system — healthcare professionals, patients, students, instructor, regulators, dataset providers, etc.

### Chapter 2 — AI Policy & Governance
This chapter is the team's formal commitment to responsible AI development. It should read like a real policy document, not like casual homework text.

- **2.1 — AI Policy Statement:** nine fields. Principles should be tied to actual tool features such as Step 7 bias detection, Step 6 SHAP, human oversight, privacy notices, etc.
- **2.2 — AI Governance Roles:** map Scrum roles to ISO 42001 governance roles. Assign real people and real responsibilities.

### Chapter 3 — Data Governance & Third-Party Management
This is the most table-heavy chapter and the one where detail matters.

- **3.1 — Data Source Inventory:** document all 20 datasets across 20 specialties.
- **3.2 — Data Quality & Validation:** explain rules for uploaded data and preprocessing choices with clinical justification.
- **3.3 — Data Privacy & Provenance:** explain browser-only processing, session-only lifecycle, provenance tracking, and dataset bias considerations.
- **3.4 — Third-Party & Dependency Register:** document external ML libraries, datasets, Docker images, frameworks, versions, licenses, security assessment, risk level, alternatives, and monitoring plan. At least 8 components.
